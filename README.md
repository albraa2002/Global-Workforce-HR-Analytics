🌍 Global Workforce & HR Analytics Dashboard

📌 Project Overview
This project transforms raw human resources data into actionable strategic insights. Acting as a Senior HR Data Analyst for "ANALYZEFORCE", I developed an interactive Python-based dashboard analyzing a simulated global workforce of 5,000 employees. The objective was not just to visualize data, but to explicitly answer 7 critical C-level business questions regarding demographics, payroll distribution, and hiring growth.

🚀 Executive Business Insights
The dashboard successfully resolved the management's key queries:
1. Active Workforce: Identified 4,244 Active Employees with a balanced gender distribution (51.2% Male, 48.8% Female).
2. Global Distribution: India represents the largest workforce hub, accounting for 40.4% of total employees.
3. Hiring Trends: The company experienced an explosive hiring growth of 285.8% YoY in the year 2024.
4. Compensation Analysis: Egypt has the lowest average salary ($18,172) compared to the overall company average ($62,028).
5. Gender Pay Gap: Revealed a structural pay gap, with active male employees earning an average of $4,635 more than their female counterparts.
6. Attendance Monitoring: Flagged specific performance risks, identifying "Alex Mercer" with a critically low attendance rate of 62.0%.
7. Payroll Allocation: Despite India having the most headcount, the USA contributes the highest total payroll cost ($94,181,757).

🛠️ Tech Stack
Language: Python 3.x
Data Manipulation: Pandas, NumPy
Data Visualization: Plotly (Interactive Bar, Pie, and Box charts)
UI/UX: Standalone HTML/CSS Grid Dashboard (Corporate Executive UI)
